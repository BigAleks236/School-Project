Walk McHenry

A formal proposal to expand sidewalk infrastructure in McHenry, Illinois to improve pedestrian safety, accessibility, and long-term community outcomes.

Overview Problem Survey Data Locations Conclusion

Project Overview

McHenry currently lacks continuous sidewalk infrastructure in several residential and high-traffic areas. This proposal recommends expanding sidewalk access to improve safety and walkability.

Problem Statement

  • Unsafe walking routes
  • Limited ADA accessibility
  • Pedestrian–vehicle conflicts

    • Studie consistently show that without sidewalks experience higher rates of pedestrian injuries and reduced accessibility.

    People are often forced to walk on road shoulders or directly in traffic lanes. this creates unsafe conditions, especially near major roads.

Priority Locations

  • Major roads
  • Parks and bus stops

    • Priority locations include areas near, major roads such as Route 31 and Bull valley Road, and community parks and bus stops

    Targeting these locations first, ensures the greatest safety impact for their investment

Cost Analysis

Sidewalk construction typically costs between $200,000 and $250,000 per mile, depending on grading, drainage, and ADA compliance. While this represents an investment, sidewalks are long-lasting infrastructure with a lifespan exceeding 30 years. Over time, they reduce accident-related costs and improve public health outcomes.”

Conclusion

Survey data shows strong concern for pedestrian safety and broad support for adding sidewalks, especially to protect children.

This proposal is about safety, accessibility, and responsible planning. By investing in sidewalk infrastructure, McHenry can protect pedestrians, support families, and build a more connected community. Sidewalks are a proven solution — and an investment in McHenry’s future.
