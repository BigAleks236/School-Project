Project Overview

McHenry currently lacks continuous sidewalk infrastructure in several residential and high-traffic areas. This proposal recommends a phased expansion of sidewalks to improve public safety, pedestrian mobility, and quality of life.

Sidewalks are not optional amenities â€” they are essential public safety infrastructure that protects residents and supports long-term city planning.

Problem Statement

Many streets in McHenry were designed primarily for vehicle traffic, leaving pedestrians without safe, dedicated walking space. As a result, residents are often forced to walk on road shoulders or directly in traffic lanes.

  • Increased risk of pedestrian accidents
  • Unsafe routes for students walking to school
  • Limited mobility for seniors and individuals with disabilities
  • Unpredictable interactions between drivers and pedestrians

Transportation safety research consistently shows that areas without sidewalks experience significantly higher pedestrian injury rates.

Accessibility, Equity, and Community Impact

Expanded sidewalk infrastructure improves mobility and access for all residents, including children, seniors, and individuals with disabilities.

  • Improved ADA compliance
  • Safer routes for students
  • Reduced vehicle dependency
  • Encouragement of physical activity
  • Stronger neighborhood connections

Walkable communities consistently report higher resident satisfaction and stronger local economies.
