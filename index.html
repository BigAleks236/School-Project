Walk McHenry

A formal proposal to expand sidewalk infrastructure in McHenry, Illinois to improve pedestrian safety, accessibility, and long-term community outcomes.

Overview Problem Locations Before & After Map Cost Impact Conclusion

Project Overview

McHenry lacks continuous sidewalk infrastructure in several residential and high-traffic areas. This proposal recommends a phased expansion of sidewalks to improve public safety, pedestrian mobility, and overall quality of life.

Sidewalks are essential public safety infrastructure and a foundational component of responsible city planning.

Problem Statement

Many streets in McHenry were designed primarily for vehicle traffic. Pedestrians are frequently forced to walk on shoulders or in traffic lanes.

  • Higher risk of pedestrian injury
  • Unsafe school walking routes
  • Limited accessibility for seniors and individuals with disabilities
  • Increased conflict between drivers and pedestrians

Priority Locations

Schools & Youth Areas

  • Landmark Elementary School

Major Roads

Community Connections

  • Bus stops
  • Parks and downtown connectors

Before & After Conditions

After

Cost Analysis

  • $30–$40 per square foot
  • $200,000–$250,000 per mile

Sidewalks are long-lasting infrastructure with a lifespan exceeding 30 years. The cost is outweighed by safety benefits and reduced emergency and healthcare expenses.

Funding Strategy

  • City capital improvement funding
  • State and federal safety grants
  • Multi-year phased construction

Accessibility & Community Impact

  • Improved ADA compliance
  • Safer school routes
  • Reduced vehicle dependence
  • Stronger neighborhood connections

Conclusion

This proposal prioritizes safety, accessibility, and responsible planning. Expanding sidewalk infrastructure is a practical, proven investment in McHenry’s future.
