üö® The Problem: Gaps in Connectivity

McHenry County has identified a lack of bicycle and pedestrian infrastructure in key corridors, which presents a safety concern and a barrier to a healthier lifestyle. Without continuous paths, residents find it difficult to walk or bike safely to local destinations.

üõ†Ô∏è Our Solution: Coordinated Infrastructure Plans

The City of McHenry and McHenry County are working together on multiple projects to improve pedestrian access, guided by the McHenry County Connection Pedestrian, Bicycle and Trails Master Plan. This plan provides a framework to develop connectivity over the next several decades.

üèóÔ∏è Current & Upcoming Sidewalk Projects

Several major projects are in various stages of development or construction in late 2025:

  • **IL Route 31 (from IL 176 to IL 120):** A project involving roadway widening and reconstruction will include new bicycle and pedestrian accommodations along the corridor. Phase II design is expected to begin soon, with construction anticipated after 2026.
  • **Bull Valley Road:** Construction is planned for a 10-foot-wide multi-use path along the north side of Bull Valley Road, connecting the Prairie Trail to IL Route 31.
  • **Randall Road Corridor:** The ongoing Phase III, Stage 2 construction, which began in March 2025, includes the installation of a new multi-use path along the east side and a new sidewalk along the west side of Randall Road.
  • **City Shared Cost Program:** The city operates a popular Shared Cost Sidewalk Program, where property owners can share the cost of repairs with the City. Applications for the 2025 program opened in January.
