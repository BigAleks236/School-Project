Walk McHenry

Sidewalk Infrastructure Improvement Proposal for the City of McHenry, Illinois

Executive Summary Community Survey Data Recommendations

Executive Summary

The City of McHenry currently experiences gaps in sidewalk connectivity within residential neighborhoods and along key pedestrian corridors. These gaps create safety concerns, limit accessibility, and reduce walkability for residents of all ages.

This proposal presents survey-based findings and recommends targeted sidewalk improvements to enhance pedestrian safety, support children and families, and align with long-term community infrastructure goals.

Community Survey Findings (Student Sample, n = 40)

A survey was conducted among 40 students attending a McHenry school to assess perceptions of sidewalk availability, pedestrian safety, and potential benefits of sidewalk expansion. While the sample is limited, the results provide insight into youth and family walking experiences within the community.

1. Perceived adequacy of sidewalks in residential areas

Adequate – 60%
Inadequate – 30%
Uncertain – 10%

2. Experience of feeling unsafe due to lack of sidewalks

Yes – 70%
No – 30%

3. Frequency of walking within neighborhood

Daily – 30%
Several times per week – 40%
Rarely – 40%

4. Belief that additional sidewalks would improve child safety

Yes – 80%
No – 20%

Recommendations and Conclusion

Survey results indicate strong concern regarding pedestrian safety and broad support for additional sidewalk infrastructure, particularly to protect children and improve neighborhood walkability.

It is recommended that the City of McHenry evaluate sidewalk gaps near schools, residential corridors, and high-traffic roadways and consider phased implementation of sidewalk improvements as part of future capital planning initiatives.
